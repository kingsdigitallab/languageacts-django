Semantic history

As with existencia the rise in frequency of existir seems not to be concerned so much with a widening of meaning or with participation in new syntagmatic environments as with what seems to be an increasing disposition towards its use, or what is sometimes called ‘shift of markedness’ (Trask 1996: 139-143). In the 19th century we find it used in three fundamental ways: (a) what might perhaps be regarded as its literal or original meaning of ‘to exist’, stressing the reality of something (example), and (b) another ‘full’ lexical meaning as the equivalent of ‘to be alive, living’ (example) and (c) what appears to be a semi-grammaticalised existential notion (example): un impedimento secreto; una dispensación condicional de un impedimento que acaso no existe (CORDE: Francisco Navarro Villoslada, Doña Urraca de Castilla, 1849) ‘a secret impediment, a conditional dispensation of an impediment which perhaps does not exist’ ¿Y mi hijo? ¡Oh! Calla; si ya no existe, no me lo digas ahora. (CORDE: Francisco Navarro Villoslada, Doña Urraca de Castilla, 1849)
‘And my son? Oh! Say nothing, if he is no longer alive, do not tell me now.’
esa misteriosa relación que entre Ramiro y Bermudo existe (CORDE: Francisco Navarro Villoslada, Doña Urraca de Castilla, 1849)
‘That mysterious relationship [which exists] between Ramiro and Bermudo’
This existential usage deserves further comment. It can be seen in (example) that Spanish uses a relative clause involving existir where in other languages no such construction needs to be used. In present-day Spanish, there is a resistance to qualifying a noun with a locative expression, and a relative clause if often used instead: thus to Eng. the man on the roof corresponds Sp. el hombre que está en el tejado. Existir is especially favoured to use to render the existential idea in such an environment:
la desconfianza que existe fuera de España hacia la tan promovida “salud del sistema bancario español” (CDE: http://decrecimientocordoba.blogspot.co.uk/2013/08/en-ocho-meses-hemos-consumido-lo-que-la.html accessed 9 January 2017)
Romário de Souza Farías ha asegurado que existe en Brasil una campaña para difamarle (CREA: La Vanguardia, 30.8.1995)
